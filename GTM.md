# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Target User
**Primary Persona:** Fractional CFOs and VP of Finance at Series A to Series C startups (50-250 employees). 
**Why:** At this stage, startups are rapidly hiring and issuing corporate cards to engineering managers to buy AI tools. The CFO sees the aggregate bill on Ramp or Brex but lacks the technical context to know if they need both Cursor Business and ChatGPT Enterprise.

## Trigger & Search Behavior
**What they search right before needing this:**
- "How to cut SaaS spend"
- "ChatGPT Enterprise vs Claude Team pricing"
- "Average AI tool spend per developer 2026"
- "Ramp vendor consolidation"

**Where they hang out online:**
- **Slack Communities:** "Off The Ledger" (Airbase's CFO community), "CFO Connect".
- **X/Twitter:** Following specific FinOps/SaaS metrics accounts (e.g., @TheSaaSCFO, @JasonLemkin).
- **Subreddits:** r/SaaS, r/startups (looking for vendor advice).

## First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Paid Budget)
1. **The "Audit Your Competitor" Wedge:** I will run the Audit Engine on the known public tech stacks of 5 major unicorns (e.g., Rippling, Vercel). I will publish these teardowns as deep-dive LinkedIn and Twitter posts: *"How Rippling is overspending by $40k/yr on AI tools..."* ending with a CTA to audit your own stack.
2. **Community Value-Add:** I will join "Off The Ledger" and offer free manual reviews of SaaS bills, using WiseBill AI in the background to generate the PDFs, naturally driving them to the tool.
3. **Product Hunt Launch:** Launching explicitly positioned as the specific FinOps tool for AI spend.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
**The "Viral Board Slide":** The PDF Export feature is specifically designed to look like a high-end consulting report. The unfair advantage is designing the output so beautifully that CFOs literally drag-and-drop the PDF into their quarterly board deck to prove they are optimizing burn rate. Every time a board member sees it, they recommend it to their other portfolio companies.

## Week-1 Traction Targets
If this works, Week-1 metrics look like:
- **1,500** Unique Visitors (driven by the Hacker News / Twitter launch).
- **450** Audits Completed (30% conversion rate from landing to completed audit).
- **60** Leads Captured (13% of completions input their email).
- **5** High-Intent Consultations booked for Techvruk.
